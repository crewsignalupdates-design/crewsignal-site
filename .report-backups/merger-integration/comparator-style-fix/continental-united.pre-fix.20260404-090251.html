Historical Comparator Reports

Continental / United

Status: Report in progress

Historical comparator on separate books, interim fence architecture, and the road to the 2016 JCBA.

United / Continental is a cautionary comparator of a different kind. Pre-merger United flight attendants were represented by CWA-AFA, while pre-merger Continental flight attendants were represented by IAMAW until the National Mediation Board certified CWA-AFA for the merged flight attendant craft or class on June 30, 2011. Even after that certification, separate subsidiary agreements remained in place and the joint United agreement was not ratified until August 12, 2016, showing that representation resolution alone did not produce immediate pay or rule parity.

That matters because United / Continental did not present the same TFP-versus-hourly conversion fight now visible in Alaska / Hawaiian. The cleaner lesson is that same-union status still did not deliver immediate pay or rule parity. Separate books remained operative until the JCBA itself resolved them, which means the architecture problem was conceptually simpler than Alaska / Hawaiian but the parity problem still lasted years.
