How to Read the LM-2

  • Statement A (Assets and Liabilities): the balance-sheet section. It shows the union’s assets, liabilities, and net assets at the start and end of the reporting period.
  • Statement B (Receipts and Disbursements): the cash-flow section. It reports cash received and cash paid out during the reporting period.
  • Representational Activities: Schedule 15. This covers bargaining preparation, contract negotiation, contract administration and enforcement, and organizing / decertification / recruitment-related representational work.
  • General Overhead: Schedule 18. This covers overhead that cannot be allocated to another Statement B disbursement category.
  • Union Administration: Schedule 19. This covers governance functions such as officer elections, regular meetings, disciplinary proceedings, trusteeships, and apprenticeship or member-education administration.
  • Benefits: Schedule 20. This covers direct and indirect benefit disbursements for officers, employees, members, and beneficiaries, including items such as health insurance, life insurance, and pensions.
  • Are these broken down in the LM-2? Yes. DOL says Form LM-2 is the most detailed annual union financial report, and Schedules 14 through 19 provide detailed information on the union’s financial operations in member-service categories.

Interpretive Limits

APFA’s single LM-2 provides a strong national-level financial picture, but it does not break member-facing representation into a CWA-AFA-style sector / MEC / LEC reporting stack. That makes APFA easier to read as one treasury, but it also means local-layer representation cannot be analyzed here through separate subordinate-unit annual reports.
