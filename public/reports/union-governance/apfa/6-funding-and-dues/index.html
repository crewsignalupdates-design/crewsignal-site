Official Filings

APFA is a single-filer union in this review. The latest official LM-2 currently available is for the fiscal year ending 03/31/2025. The FY ending 03/31/2026 filing is not yet due.

  • FY ending 03/31/2025: Official filing
  • FY ending 03/31/2024: Official filing

Filing-status note: annual LM reports are due within 90 days after fiscal year-end. For APFA’s FY ending 03/31/2026, the due date is 06/29/2026.

Governance Basics (Items 10–21)

The annual APFA LM-2 provides a stable governance snapshot across the last three years: PAC activity is reported, outside audit/review is reported, the fidelity bond is $500,000, and the regular dues rate remains $41 per month.

  • FY2025: PAC yes; outside audit yes; fidelity bond $500,000; next regular election 02/2027; 28,732 members; regular dues $41 per month.
  • FY2024: PAC yes; outside audit yes; fidelity bond $500,000; next regular election 02/2028; 26,523 members; regular dues $41 per month.
  • FY2023: PAC yes; outside audit yes; fidelity bond $500,000; next regular election 02/2024; 25,085 members; regular dues $41 per month.

Notable Schedule Signals

APFA’s LM-2 is especially useful where it surfaces carrier-connected balance-sheet items. The recurring American Airlines Payloss entries are one of the clearest examples.

  • FY2025: American Airlines Payloss appears as $18,118 in accounts receivable and $1,075,650 in accounts payable.
  • FY2024: American Airlines Payloss appears as $94,662 in accounts receivable and $1,516,786 in accounts payable.
  • FY2023: American Airlines Payloss appears as $216,311 in accounts receivable and $1,241,673 in accounts payable.

For APFA, this kind of schedule-level signal is often more analytically useful than trying to force a local-subordinate map that does not exist in the filing structure.

Interpretive Limits

APFA’s single LM-2 gives a strong national-level financial picture, but it does not break member-facing representation into a CWA-AFA-style sector / MEC / LEC reporting stack. That makes APFA easier to read as one treasury, but it also means local-layer representation cannot be analyzed here through separate subordinate-unit annual reports.
