Transport Workers Union

Financial Transparency & LM Reporting

International per-capita structure, local flight-attendant funding, and carrier-focused financial disclosures for Southwest, Allegiant, Flight Services International, and JetBlue.

Financial Disclosures

TWU’s finances should be read in two layers. The International books the top-level per-capita and affiliate-transmittal structure, while the flight-attendant groups reviewed here are organized through identifiable locals.

That makes TWU easier to follow than a parent/sector system like CWA-AFA. The key question for readers is not just what TWU International receives, but how much financial and representational capacity is visible at the flight-attendant local level.

How the Money Flow Works

TWU International’s LM-2 shows that the International does not book direct dues and agency fees as its main recurring labor-organization income. Instead, the principal top-level recurring income shown in the reviewed filings is per-capita tax, together with funds collected on behalf of affiliates for transmittal to them.

  • International layer: per-capita tax, international administration, and funds collected or transmitted for affiliates.
  • Local layer: carrier-facing flight-attendant representation through Local 556, Local 577, Local 578, and Local 579.
  • Member-facing question: whether local funding and local filing visibility make it easier to see what dues are buying in day-to-day representation and contract enforcement.

TWU International Filings

The International filings are the right place to show TWU’s top-level scale and the degree to which the International acts as a clearing and transmittal layer for affiliates.

  • FY ending 08/31/2025: TWU International LM-2 — members 157,778; per-capita tax $35,787,812; funds collected on behalf of affiliates for transmittal to them $27,679,831; total receipts $67,535,161; representational activities $8,407,755.
  • FY ending 08/31/2024: TWU International LM-2 — members 153,682; per-capita tax $31,734,160; funds collected on behalf of affiliates for transmittal to them $24,093,546; total receipts $58,990,512; representational activities $7,902,546.
  • FY ending 08/31/2023: TWU International LM-2 — members 148,101; per-capita tax $29,502,947; funds collected on behalf of affiliates for transmittal to them $22,486,095; total receipts $56,090,321; representational activities $9,010,041.

The International filings show a clearer top-level pass-through structure than CWA-AFA’s parent/sector split, but the most important member-facing representation still sits at the local level.

TWU Cabin Crew Coalition Structure

TWU’s official Cabin Crew Coalition publicly groups together the flight-attendant locals that matter most for this analysis.

  • Southwest Airlines: Local 556
  • Allegiant Air: Local 577
  • Flight Services International: Local 578
  • JetBlue: Local 579

Southwest Airlines — Local 556

Southwest is the clearest current TWU example of directly visible local flight-attendant funding. Local 556 files its own LM-2 and explicitly identifies its unit as “AIR TRANSPORT SOUTHWEST AIRLINES FLIGHT ATTENDANTS.”

  • Official local site: TWU Local 556
  • FY ending 09/30/2025: Local 556 LM-2 — unit name “AIR TRANSPORT SOUTHWEST AIRLINES FLIGHT ATTENDANTS”; members 21,598; regular dues $61 per month; total receipts $14,429,069; representational activities $7,225,524; per-capita tax $4,297,662.
  • Verified historical snapshots: FY ending 09/30/2023 — members 20,971; regular dues $50 per month; total receipts $12,639,998; representational activities $6,770,099. | FY ending 09/30/2022 — members 18,569; regular dues $50 per month; total receipts $10,714,533; representational activities $5,679,981.
  • Current contract and bargaining source: 2024 contract ratification release

For CrewSignal readers, Local 556 is the strongest current TWU example of a local filing that visibly links dues, receipts, and representational spending to a named flight-attendant bargaining unit.

Allegiant Air — Local 577

Allegiant flight attendants are represented by Local 577. TWU says Local 577 represents 1,700 Allegiant flight attendants and ratified a new five-year contract in 2024.

  • Official TWU release: 2024 contract ratification release
  • Contract PDF: Allegiant Flight Attendant contract
  • Verified International funding signals: TWU International reported total transactions with Local 577 of $43,281 in FY2023, $13,177 in FY2024, and $86,381 in FY2025, largely labeled as campaign-expense or organizing-wage reimbursements.
  • Standalone local filing: No verified standalone Local 577 LM filing is linked here yet.

Flight Services International — Local 578

Flight Services International flight attendants are represented by Local 578, a Houston-based TWU local.

  • Official local site: TWU Local 578
  • Official TWU release: 2025 contract ratification release
  • Dues guidance: Local 578’s official dues FAQ says dues are $26.00 per month and that, under the TWU Constitution, 70% of dues are turned back over to the local to meet local expenses.
  • Dues FAQ: Local 578 dues FAQ
  • Standalone local filing: No verified standalone Local 578 LM filing is linked here yet.

JetBlue — Local 579

JetBlue flight attendants are represented by Local 579, whose official site identifies itself as the union of JetBlue flight attendants.

  • Official local site: TWU Local 579
  • Dues guidance: Local 579’s official dues page says dues were $35.00 per month and, effective June 1, 2025, are $48.36 per month. The same page says that, under the TWU Constitution, 70% of dues are turned back over to the local.
  • Dues FAQ: Local 578 dues FAQ
  • Verified International funding signals: TWU International reported a $100,000 “start up” transaction to Local 579 in FY2023, $334,465 in organizing-wage reimbursements in FY2024, and $162,841 in campaign-expense reimbursements in FY2025.
  • Standalone local filing: No verified standalone Local 579 LM filing is linked here yet.

Important Limit

TWU is easier to follow than a multi-layered structure like CWA-AFA in one important respect: the flight-attendant groups reviewed here sit in identifiable locals, and at least one of those locals — Local 556 — files a clearly labeled flight-attendant LM-2.

But not every local filing has been independently verified in the reviewed public record yet. For Allegiant, FSI, and JetBlue, this page therefore combines verified official local and carrier sources with verified TWU International financial signals, while avoiding claims that a standalone local filing has been confirmed when it has not.
