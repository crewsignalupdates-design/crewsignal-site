Funding, Dues, and Financial Governance

How the International Association of Machinists and Aerospace Workers funds operations, allocates resources, and structures financial oversight across a multi-industry governance model.

Overview

This section describes the union’s funding architecture at a structural level: how dues and related revenues support operations, how spending authority is organized, and what reporting frameworks provide visibility into major categories of disbursement.

Structural Signals

  • Dues and revenue design: how member contributions are structured and routed through the organization.
  • Spending authority: where authorization resides and how expenditures are approved or constrained.
  • Oversight posture: what internal controls and external disclosures reveal about financial governance.

Statutory Financial Disclosure (LM-2)

The U.S. Department of Labor requires many labor organizations to file annual financial disclosure reports under the Labor-Management Reporting and Disclosure Act (LMRDA). LM-2 filings provide a standardized view of revenue sources, disbursement categories, officer compensation, and major spending categories.
