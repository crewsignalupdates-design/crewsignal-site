Funding and Dues Flow

How member dues move through the system — what each layer retains, what it funds, and how filings can be used to verify.

1. The Foundational Detail

AFA dues are assessed as a flat monthly amount, rather than as a percentage of gross wages. At the time of writing, the commonly cited rate is $50 per month. This flat-rate approach distinguishes AFA from many unions whose dues scale directly with income.

This report focuses on structural flow and allocation. Dollar amounts and percentages should always be verified against current governing documents and LM filings.

2. Dues Flow Upward

Member dues flow upward from local councils to AFA–CWA and then to CWA National through per-capita mechanisms. While exact allocations vary by year and policy, the overall structure follows a consistent pattern.

Approximate Retention by Level (Conceptual)

Level Funding Source Approx. Retention Range Primary Use (High-Level)
Local Unions / LECs Monthly member dues ~20–25% Member representation, casework, local operations, communications
AFA–CWA (Sector / International) Per-capita from locals ~45–50% System-wide bargaining, safety programs, legal support, national communications
CWA National Per-capita from sectors ~25–30% Organizing, political and legislative programs, audits, national infrastructure

Percentages are presented as an illustrative model. Year-specific allocations should be confirmed using LM filings and official financial disclosures.

3. Why Retention Matters

Retention levels shape the functional capacity of each layer. Local retention affects day-to-day representation and responsiveness. AFA retention supports national bargaining, industry programs, and cross-carrier coordination. CWA National retention funds broader organizing efforts, political activity, and compliance infrastructure.

Later reports in this series will use verified filings to compare how different flight attendant unions allocate resources across representation, bargaining, and enforcement.

4. Verification Path

The primary public verification tools for union funding and expenditures are the Department of Labor’s LM filings: LM-2 (large organizations), LM-3, and LM-4 (smaller organizations).

CrewSignal maintains a dedicated filings library with direct links and summarized extracts for reference and comparison.
