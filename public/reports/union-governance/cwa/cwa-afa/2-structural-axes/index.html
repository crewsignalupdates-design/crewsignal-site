Authority Framework

Authority within CWA-AFA is not autonomous. It is exercised pursuant to powers delegated by the Communications Workers of America (CWA) and is constrained by the CWA Constitution, national governance bodies, and parent-union administrative control.

CWA-AFA functions as an operational and representational layer rather than as an independent source of constitutional authority.

Origination of Authority

Formal authority originates at the CWA national level. Constitutional authority is vested in the CWA membership collectively and exercised through national conventions, elected national officers, and the CWA Executive Board.

CWA-AFA leadership positions, councils, and committees derive authority through charter and delegation rather than direct constitutional grant from the flight attendant membership.

Flow of Authority

Authority flows downward from the CWA national structure to sector-level bodies such as CWA-AFA, and further to carrier councils, base representatives, and committee structures.

  • National level: constitutional interpretation, financial control, and ultimate governance authority.
  • Sector level: delegated authority for industry-specific representation and coordination.
  • Carrier and base level: operational representation within defined limits.

Delegated authority may be modified, withdrawn, or overridden by the parent union in accordance with constitutional and administrative provisions.

Termination and Override Authority

Final decision-making authority resides with the CWA national union. The CWA Executive Board and national officers retain the ability to override sector-level decisions, impose administrative control, or resolve disputes affecting governance, finance, or compliance.

Sector-level decisions do not constitute final authority where they conflict with national policy, constitutional interpretation, or statutory compliance requirements.

Member Influence and Control Points

Flight attendants affiliated with CWA-AFA exercise influence primarily through local participation, carrier councils, and sector-level representation mechanisms. Direct control over national authority structures is indirect and mediated through the parent unionâ€™s governance processes.

Critical control points include delegate selection, internal appeals, and participation in sector-level governance bodies operating under CWA oversight.

Structural Implications

  • Sector-level authority is derivative rather than sovereign.
  • Ultimate governance control resides with the parent union.
  • Operational autonomy exists within defined and revocable limits.
  • Member influence is strongest at the local and carrier level.
