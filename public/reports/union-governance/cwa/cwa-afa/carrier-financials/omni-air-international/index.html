Omni Air International

Tracing money from parent CWA to the CWA-AFA sector to the visible Omni MEC and local layers.

Quick Read

  • Visible structure: Omni is a mixed but useful comparator because the public record shows visible CWA-AFA sector funding to both the Omni MEC and LEC 26072.
  • Most recent verified standalone filing signal: Omni LEC 26072 LM-4 (FY ending 05/31/2021) reports 299 members, $8,082 receipts, $8,082 disbursements, and $3,936 payments to officers and employees.
  • Visible direct local share: 10.5% in FY2025, 13.3% in FY2024, and 47.3% in FY2023.
  • Bottom line: Omni shows a visible local-dollar throughline, but the local share remains relatively modest in FY2025 and FY2024.

Filing Map

Omni has to be read in layers. Parent CWA is the upstream dues layer. The CWA-AFA sector LM-2 is the clearest public source for visible Omni affiliate funding. A standalone Omni local filing adds a historical local-layer spending signal, and the elections record shows an active local council.

  • CWA-AFA sector filings: FY ending 05/31/2025; FY ending 05/31/2024; FY ending 05/31/2023.

Visible CWA-AFA Sector Funding

  • FY2025: AFA MEC Omni Air International $60,418; AFA LEC 26072 Omni Air International $7,057.
  • FY2024: AFA MEC Omni Air International $48,975; AFA LEC 26072 Omni Air International $7,510.
  • FY2023: AFA MEC Omni Air International $125,432; AFA LEC 26072 Omni Air International $112,515.

What the Visible Direct Local Share Means

  • FY2025: 7,057 ÷ (60,418 + 7,057) = 10.5%.
  • FY2024: 7,510 ÷ (48,975 + 7,510) = 13.3%.
  • FY2023: 112,515 ÷ (125,432 + 112,515) = 47.3%.

Treat this as a floor, not a complete service measure.
