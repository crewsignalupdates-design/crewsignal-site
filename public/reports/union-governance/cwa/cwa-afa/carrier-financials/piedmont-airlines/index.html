Piedmont Airlines

Tracing money from parent CWA to the CWA-AFA sector to the visible Piedmont MEC and local layers.

Quick Read

  • Visible structure: Piedmont is one of the clearer CWA-AFA carriers because the public record shows visible CWA-AFA sector funding to the Piedmont MEC and to a named Piedmont local body, LEC 22061.
  • Most recent verified standalone filing signal: Piedmont LEC 22061 LM-3 (FY ending 05/31/2025) reports 353 members, $54,834 in total receipts, and $54,834 in total disbursements.
  • Visible direct local share: 81.9% in FY2025 and 91.1% in FY2024. FY2023 local-share math is not finalized here pending a cleaner verification of the 2023 local line.
  • Bottom line: Piedmont provides a strong local-dollar throughline because the public record clearly shows both sector-to-MEC and sector-to-local funding, plus a current standalone local filing.

Visible CWA-AFA Sector Funding

  • FY2025: AFA MEC Piedmont Airlines $30,849; AFA LEC 22061 Piedmont Airlines $139,443.
  • FY2024: AFA MEC Piedmont Airlines $5,572; AFA LEC 22061 Piedmont Airlines $57,229.
  • FY2023: AFA MEC Piedmont Airlines $26,948; AFA LEC 22061 Piedmont Airlines is visibly itemized in the FY2023 sector filing, but the local amount is not finalized on this page until it is rechecked cleanly.

These are visible CWA-AFA sector allocations to Piedmont-identified affiliates. They are not, by themselves, a complete measure of all bargaining, legal, administrative, or parent-supported service delivered to the property.

What the Visible Direct Local Share Means

This metric measures the percentage of visible Piedmont-specific CWA-AFA sector funding that went directly to the named Piedmont local body rather than to the Piedmont MEC.

Formula: direct named Piedmont local funding ÷ total visible Piedmont-specific CWA-AFA sector funding.

  • FY2025: 139,443 ÷ (30,849 + 139,443) = 81.9%.
  • FY2024: 57,229 ÷ (5,572 + 57,229) = 91.1%.
  • FY2023: not finalized here until the FY2023 Piedmont local line is rechecked cleanly.

Treat this as a floor, not a complete service measure. It captures only direct Piedmont-specific CWA-AFA sector funding lines that are publicly visible on the sector LM-2.
