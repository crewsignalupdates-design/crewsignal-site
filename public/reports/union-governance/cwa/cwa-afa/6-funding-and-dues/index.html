Parent-union money flow, sector-level financial reporting, and carrier-by-carrier MEC and LEC filing directories.

Financial Disclosures

CWA-AFA’s finances must be read in layers. The parent CWA filing captures the upstream dues base, the sector filing captures sector-level deployment and redistribution, and the MEC / LEC filings show how money reaches the airline and local representation layers.

This page keeps those layers in one place. The top sections explain the money flow and identify the most important sector-level financial signals. The carrier directory below links to the airline-level MEC and LEC filings that show how representation is funded on the ground.

How the Money Flow Works

CWA-AFA is not a single-filer financial structure. The parent CWA books the main dues base, the sector books deployment and redistribution, and airline MECs and LECs show how money reaches the member-facing representation layer.

  • Parent CWA layer: upstream dues routing, parent-level disbursements, and sector funding.
  • CWA-AFA sector layer: sector-wide deployment, officer compensation, property, overhead, and airline funding.
  • MEC / LEC layer: airline-specific and local-level representation, grievances, administration, and member service.

The final value-per-dues-dollar analysis depends on reading all three layers together and removing inter-affiliate double counting.

Important Reporting Limitation: Employer-Paid Release Time

Public LM filings do not always capture the full economic value associated with union office. In airline unions, officers and representatives may perform union business while remaining on company payroll, or they may be compensated through arrangements that are only partly visible on union financial reports.

  • Union-paid or union-reimbursed release time: if the union pays lost time directly or reimburses the airline for wages paid while an officer or representative is on union business, those amounts should appear on the union’s LM report.
  • Employer-paid no-docking or union leave: if the airline continues to pay the individual directly and the union does not reimburse the airline, that value may leave little or no direct trail on the union’s LM report.
  • Why this matters for readers: a low or zero salary line for an officer does not necessarily mean the office carried little or no economic benefit. Some of that value may be visible only indirectly, or may not be visible on the union’s LM filing at all.

In the United filings, some of this activity appears to be visible through entries labeled “REIMBURSE LOCAL FOR FPL WAGES” and “FPL ADMIN FEES.” Preserve the filing terminology unless and until the underlying agreement is separately verified.

DOL guidance says lost time paid by the union should be reported on LM forms, including Schedule 11 or 12, Column D on Form LM-2. But employer-paid “union leave” and “no-docking” arrangements may leave little or no direct trail on the union’s LM report when the union does not reimburse the employer.
