Communications Workers of America

Funding and Dues

How the union is funded, how dues revenue is generally allocated, and where federally filed LM-2 disclosures can be accessed for standardized financial review.

Dues Structure Overview

Members of the Communications Workers of America (CWA) fund the union’s activities through regular dues assessments. Dues are generally established at the national level, with additional assessments determined by sectoral, district, or local structures depending on the bargaining unit and affiliate arrangement.

Dues revenue supports the union’s core representational, administrative, and operational functions across its national and subordinate bodies.

Allocation of Dues Revenue

Dues collected by CWA are allocated across a range of functions, including:

  • Collective bargaining and contract administration
  • Representation in grievances, arbitration, and related proceedings
  • Organizing and membership development
  • Administrative operations and staffing
  • Legal, professional, and consulting services
  • Transfers and per-capita payments to affiliates and subordinate bodies, where applicable

The specific distribution of dues revenue varies by year and is reflected in the union’s federally filed financial reports.

Financial Transparency and Reporting

As a labor organization subject to the Labor-Management Reporting and Disclosure Act (LMRDA), CWA is required to file annual financial disclosures with the U.S. Department of Labor.

These filings are intended to provide transparency into the union’s finances, including revenues, expenditures, assets, liabilities, and payments to officers and outside entities.

LM-2 Financial Disclosures

CWA files Form LM-2, the most detailed financial disclosure required under the LMRDA. LM-2 reports provide comprehensive information, including:

  • Total dues revenue and other income
  • Disbursements by functional category
  • Officer salaries and other compensation
  • Political activities and lobbying expenditures
  • Legal and professional fees
  • Transfers to affiliates and subordinate bodies
  • Assets, liabilities, and reserves

LM-2 filings serve as the primary source for analyzing union finances on a standardized and comparable basis.
