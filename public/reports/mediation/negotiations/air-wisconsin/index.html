Air Wisconsin

NMB Docket: A-14050 · Mediator: Anthony Tosi

Amendable: 2022.10.01 · Last updated: 2026.03.23

Status Summary

Air Wisconsin’s current flight-attendant agreement became amendable on 2022.10.01. Official public sources still place the case in mediation: Harbor’s 2023 annual report says the flight-attendant agreement became amendable in October 2022 and is in mediated negotiations, and the National Mediation Board’s FY2026 budget justification separately lists Air Wisconsin flight attendants among cases that remain in mediation.

The biggest new public development is corporate rather than procedural. Harbor disclosed in a January 2026 8-K that it completed the sale and disposition of Air Wisconsin-related operating assets to CSI Aviation on 2026.01.09 and, after the sale, neither Harbor nor its subsidiaries retained material operating assets or infrastructure to support an airline. Third-party reporting later tied that ownership change to recall notices, a reshaped flying pattern, and a diminished public passenger-network profile.

  • Official posture: Public company and NMB materials still place the file in mediation.
  • Ownership reset: Harbor closed the sale on 2026.01.09 and exited airline operating infrastructure.
  • What matters next: A confirmed bargaining restart, tentative-agreement path, or formal NMB/company signal under the new ownership structure.

CrewSignal Watch Points

The key update on Air Wisconsin is that the mediation file now sits inside a materially different operating context. The bargaining status did not disappear, but the business assumptions surrounding staffing, flying, and leverage likely changed once CSI took over the operating platform.

Public bargaining detail is still thin. The only public procedural detail CrewSignal identified on bargaining resumption is a January 28 CWA-AFA update saying returning flight attendants received an immediate pay raise and that Section 6 negotiations were expected to resume within 60 to 90 days. Because that detail has not been independently confirmed by a company filing or NMB release, CrewSignal treats it as a useful but limited procedural signal.

  • Mediation track: Officially active.
  • Operating context: New ownership and a changed flying pattern now matter to how the file should be read.
  • Evidence gap: The next high-signal update still needs company, NMB, or independent confirmation.

Strike Vote Context

Strike-authorization messaging has been part of the Air Wisconsin pressure campaign, but CrewSignal treats any strike vote as a pressure signal, not as a legal countdown to job action.

Under the Railway Labor Act, lawful self-help can occur only after the National Mediation Board determines mediation has been exhausted, issues a proffer of arbitration, the parties are released from mediation if that proffer is rejected, and the statutory cooling-off process runs without a settlement or further federal intervention.
