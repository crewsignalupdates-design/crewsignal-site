Frontier Airlines

Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14096 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher Amendable: 2024.05.15 · Last updated: 2026.03.25

Status Summary

Frontier’s Flight Attendant agreement became amendable on 2024.05.15. Official company and National Mediation Board materials still place the case in formal mediation: Frontier’s 2025 Form 10-K says CWA-AFA filed for mediation through the National Mediation Board in October 2024, that the agreement remains amendable, and that the parties have been meeting monthly since February 2025; the NMB’s FY2026 budget justification separately lists Frontier Flight Attendants among cases that remain in mediation.

CrewSignal did not identify a newer non-union/public bargaining report that changes that posture. The best current read still comes from Frontier’s own filing plus NMB materials rather than from new article-by-article bargaining coverage.

  • Official posture: Frontier is in formal mediation, not just post-amendable direct bargaining.
  • Cadence: The company says the parties have been meeting monthly since February 2025.
  • What matters next: A mediated tentative agreement, a changed NMB posture, or a materially different company disclosure.

CrewSignal Watch Points

Frontier’s file remains one of the clearest examples of a case where official status is stronger than public tactical detail. Reuters documented the original dispute over the carrier’s operating-model overhaul in spring 2024, and Frontier’s 2025 Form 10-K now confirms that the dispute lives inside formal NMB mediation.

What has not emerged is a newer independent public bargaining readout that materially narrows timing or package shape. Until that changes, CrewSignal should keep the page anchored to the official mediation posture rather than trying to infer late-stage progress.

  • Mediation track: Officially active.
  • Negotiation risk: Visibility into the current package shape remains limited.
  • Next inflection point: Watch for a mediated tentative agreement, ratification calendar, or formal NMB step.

Strike Vote Context

Public reporting in September 2024 said Frontier Flight Attendants voted 99.6% to authorize a strike. CrewSignal treats that as a pressure signal, not as a legal countdown to job action.

Under the Railway Labor Act, a strike vote does not itself authorize a strike. Lawful self-help can occur only after the National Mediation Board has exhausted mediation, issued a proffer of arbitration, released the parties if that proffer is rejected, and the statutory cooling-off period has run.
