Spirit Airlines

Restructuring-related contract status under the Railway Labor Act.

Original CBA amendable: 2026.01.01 · Last updated: 2026.03.10

Status Summary

Spirit’s original 2023 Flight Attendant agreement became amendable in January 2026, but the public record no longer supports treating this as a standard open Section 6 bargaining cycle. In late 2025, Spirit and its Flight Attendants reached restructuring-related labor amendments tied to the carrier’s Chapter 11 process, and Spirit announced on 2025.12.12 that the Flight Attendants had ratified those agreements.

Reporting said the revised flight-attendant contract took effect on 2026.01.01 and included cost-reduction provisions related to overtime, ground-hold pay, and scheduling. That means the story is now less about an ordinary post-amendable bargaining round and more about the practical effects of a ratified restructuring deal while Spirit works toward emerging from bankruptcy.

  • Process posture: Spirit is no longer a clean active-negotiations page; it is now a restructuring-related contract page.
  • Key change: The Flight Attendants ratified amended labor terms in December 2025, with the revised contract taking effect on 2026.01.01.
  • What matters next: The next high-signal markers are bankruptcy emergence, implementation effects, and any later reopening of ordinary bargaining.

CrewSignal Watch Points

Spirit should likely come off the active Negotiations list on the hub once you are ready to repaste it. The stronger public record now points to a ratified, bankruptcy-related contract modification rather than a typical open amendable round.

The company’s February 2026 restructuring announcement also matters here. It said Spirit had reached agreement in principle with its secured creditors and expected to emerge from Chapter 11 in late spring or early summer, which means the labor story is still intertwined with the broader restructuring timetable.

  • Negotiation track: Superseded in the near term by restructuring-related contract changes.
  • Visibility risk: The public record is much clearer on the restructuring deal than on any new post-2026 bargaining cycle.
  • Next inflection point: Watch for bankruptcy-court milestones, emergence timing, and any later return to normal amendable bargaining.

Strike Vote Context

CrewSignal did not identify a reliable non-union public reference to a current Spirit Flight Attendant strike-authorization vote tied to this 2025–2026 restructuring cycle.

Where a strike vote does occur, CrewSignal treats it as a pressure signal, not as a legal countdown to job action. Under the Railway Labor Act, a strike vote does not itself authorize a strike. Lawful self-help can occur only after the National Mediation Board has exhausted mediation, released the parties following a proffer of arbitration, and the statutory cooling-off process has run.
