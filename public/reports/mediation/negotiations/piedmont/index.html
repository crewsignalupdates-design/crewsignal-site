Piedmont Airlines

Section 6 bargaining under the Railway Labor Act.

Amendable: 2026.03.03 · Last updated: 2026.03.24

Status Summary

Piedmont’s current Flight Attendant agreement runs through 2026.03.02, making 2026.03.03 the first amendable day under the Railway Labor Act. American’s 2025 Form 10-K separately lists Piedmont Flight Attendants as a represented group with a 2026 contract amendable date.

CrewSignal did not identify a notable non-union/public bargaining report, company statement, or public National Mediation Board mediation reference specific to Piedmont Flight Attendants that materially clarifies the current bargaining posture.

The clearest current procedural trail is union-side. The public CWA-AFA Piedmont negotiations page links a November 17, 2025 opening proposal and January-February 2026 updates, including a February 15 item whose title says 12 sections had been tentatively agreed. That supports an active post-amendable bargaining read, but not a publicly documented tentative-agreement path or mediation posture.

  • Contract posture: The agreement became amendable on 2026.03.03.
  • Procedural signal: Public union-side updates indicate active early-round bargaining and tentative agreements on 12 sections as of 2026.02.15.
  • Visibility risk: No notable non-union/public bargaining-specific update was identified for this update.

CrewSignal Watch Points

The public opener shows a substantive economic and work-rule round rather than a narrow cleanup round. Public proposals seek higher pay rates, a duty rig, boarding pay, sit pay, retro pay, a signing or retention bonus, stronger sick-leave language, better per diem and hotel standards, more days off, reserve reform, insurance improvements, and a higher 401(k) match.

At the same time, the opener keeps Recognition & Scope as current book and proposes a three-year duration. That points to a broad compensation and quality-of-life bargaining round, but not an opener built around a structural rewrite of the scope article.

Without non-union/public reporting or public National Mediation Board mediation visibility specific to this flight-attendant file, CrewSignal should stay disciplined about describing the case as active negotiations rather than something more advanced.

  • Negotiation track: Active post-amendable bargaining.
  • Package shape: The public opener points to broad economics, scheduling, reserve, and benefits issues.
  • Next inflection point: A company statement, independent third-party report, public National Mediation Board mediation reference, or tentative agreement announcement.
