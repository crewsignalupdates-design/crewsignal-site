CrewSignal · Mediation Status

Avelo Airlines

Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14061 · Mediator: Kevin Barry Amendable: N/A — first contract · Last updated: 2026.03.25

Status Summary

Avelo Flight Attendants do not yet have an amendable date because this remains a first-contract case. Official National Mediation Board materials place Avelo among Flight Attendant cases that remain in mediation, and the Board’s 2022 determinations page shows the certification that established CWA-AFA representation effective 2022.04.14.

The biggest new public development is operational. On 2026.01.06, Avelo announced that it would streamline its network around four current bases, open Dallas / McKinney later in 2026, close Mesa, Raleigh-Durham, and Wilmington as bases, remove six 737-700s, and focus on five core bases. Third-party reporting the next day said Avelo would also end ICE deportation flights at the end of January, underscoring how quickly the operating backdrop is changing around first-contract mediation.

  • Process posture: Official sources still place Avelo in first-contract mediation.
  • Amendable date: Not applicable until a first ratified Flight Attendant CBA exists.
  • Operating reset: Early-2026 network and fleet changes materially changed the business backdrop.

CrewSignal Watch Points

The best current read on Avelo is still procedural rather than article-by-article. Mediation remains the official status, but the carrier’s network reset means bargaining is taking place against a changing footprint, fleet mix, and base structure.

Public bargaining detail outside union communications remains thin. That means CrewSignal can confidently describe the case as active first-contract mediation, but not as a case with a clearly documented tentative-agreement timeline or release sequence.

  • Mediation track: Officially active.
  • Business context: Base closures and fleet simplification may shape bargaining assumptions.
  • Evidence gap: No notable non-union/public bargaining-specific readout materially updated contract text or timing.

Strike Vote Context

CrewSignal did not identify reliable non-union/public reporting of a strike-authorization vote by Avelo Flight Attendants in the sources reviewed for this update.

If a strike vote does occur, CrewSignal would treat it as a pressure signal, not as a legal strike trigger. Under the Railway Labor Act, lawful self-help can occur only after the National Mediation Board has exhausted mediation, issued a proffer of arbitration, released the parties if that proffer is rejected, and the statutory cooling-off process has run.
