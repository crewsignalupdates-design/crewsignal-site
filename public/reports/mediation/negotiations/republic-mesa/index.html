üßæ Joint / Shared Representation

Republic flight attendants are represented by IBT Local 135, while Mesa flight attendants are represented by CWA-AFA. As a result, the interim JCBA required not only negotiations with the company, but also coordination and agreement between the two unions regarding structure, scope, and duration.

This joint representation arrangement is distinct from standard single-union bargaining and from traditional JCBAs negotiated under a single representative organization.

Public descriptions of the process indicate that the interim JCBA was designed to function as a bridge agreement, preserving stability while deferring full harmonization to a later Section 6 bargaining cycle.

The Republic collective bargaining agreement was used as the baseline template for the interim JCBA, allowing the parties to move quickly while avoiding prolonged disputes over foundational structure. Differences between the legacy agreements are addressed in detail in the Contract Architecture section.

In addition to company bargaining, the process required union-to-union negotiation to determine how the interim agreement would be administered, how representation would function during the interim period, and how future bargaining rights would be preserved.
