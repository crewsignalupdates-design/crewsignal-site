üß≠ First Contract Bargaining Process (Context)

First contracts commonly take extended time because the parties must build an entire agreement from the ground up (recognition, scope, scheduling/work rules, compensation, benefits, grievance/arbitration, and union rights), rather than modifying an already-amendable agreement.

Public Breeze AFA communications reflect early first-contract steps such as member surveys, committee formation, and negotiation training, which typically precede or run alongside initial proposal exchange and section-by-section bargaining.
