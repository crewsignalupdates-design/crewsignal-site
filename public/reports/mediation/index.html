United / CWA-AFA Mediation

United Airlines flight attendants, represented by the CWA-AFA, remain in Railway Labor Act (RLA) mediation under National Mediation Board (NMB) oversight. Following the rejection of the May 2025 Tentative Agreement in July 2025, the parties returned to structured talks focused on targeted work-rule and quality-of-life improvements layered on top of the prior economic framework. As of the latest year-to-date status cut (through 2025.11.14), there has been no NMB release into a 30-day cooling-off period and no declared strike deadline.

CrewSignal continues to monitor for:

  • Any shift in NMB posture signaling heightened pressure or movement toward release;
  • Evidence of convergence or breakdown in the targeted “TA2” pathway;
  • Escalation versus de-escalation signals, including mobilization activity or expanded bargaining dates;
  • Contract-architecture inflection points—especially scheduling, reserve rules, hotels, benefits thresholds, and enforcement language—that drive ratification risk.
