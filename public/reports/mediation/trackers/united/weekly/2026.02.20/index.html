United / JetBlue (Blue Sky) — Weekly Update

Week ending: 2026.02.20

Status This Week

  • Customer-facing “cross-airline booking” is live for eligible single-carrier itineraries across both airlines’ web and app booking channels.
  • This remains a commercial/loyalty integration track (not an operational merger): each carrier continues to publish and operate its own schedule.
  • Near-term integration focus appears to be stabilizing cross-sell workflows (pricing, ticketing, changes, IRROPS handling) and expanding eligible markets/itineraries.

Process Signals

  • Public launch messaging indicates a meaningful back-end milestone: reciprocal booking channels require systems connectivity (inventory display, ticketing/settlement rules, and loyalty currency handling).
  • Continued emphasis on “phased rollout” suggests additional feature releases (expanded earn/redeem, recognition benefits, and broader itinerary eligibility) are still pending.
  • Early phase launches are typically followed by a stabilization window; absence of new announcements is not necessarily a stall signal.

CrewSignal Assessment

  • This week reads as an “integration milestone” rather than a “policy/structure change” event.
  • Watch for next signals that the partnership is broadening beyond single-carrier itineraries (e.g., mixed itineraries, expanded recognition benefits, cross-carrier day-of-travel handling).
  • If integration accelerates, the operational knock-on risks tend to surface in IRROPS, misconnect handling, and customer-service load during transition phases.
