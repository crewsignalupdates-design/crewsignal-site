CrewSignal · Weekly Mediation Update

United Airlines (CWA-AFA)

NMB Docket: A-14065 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher Week ending: 2026.02.13

Status This Week

United and CWA-AFA met in federal mediation in Chicago during the week (February 10–12) as the parties continued work toward a revised tentative agreement. Public union communications described the talks as being in a late-stage posture, with fewer than a dozen open issues remaining, and indicated the next scheduled mediation sessions are in March.

There was no public indication this week of a National Mediation Board release into a 30‑day cooling‑off period, and no strike deadline was announced.

Key Process Signals

  • Mediation sessions occurred: CWA-AFA reported meeting February 10–12 in Chicago under federal mediation.
  • Endgame framing: CWA-AFA described progress on non‑economic language earlier in the cycle and characterized remaining issues as major quality‑of‑life/work‑rule items with economic cost.
  • Economic baseline pressure: CWA-AFA stated the company’s position is that overall economics would match the prior tentative agreement, while the union emphasized “clean, targeted improvements” without offsetting concessions (explicitly rejecting tradeoffs such as eliminating PTO).
  • Next scheduled dates: CWA-AFA listed two remaining scheduled mediation blocks: March 3–6 and March 24–27.
  • NMB administrative posture: The NMB Weekly Activity Report for February 9–13 reported no new mediation dockets and no mediation case closures for the week.

CrewSignal Assessment

This week reads as a “late-stage mediation” interval: bargaining occurred, both sides appear to be working inside a constrained economic framework, and the remaining dispute centers on quality‑of‑life/work‑rule items that carry cost and operational implications. The most meaningful forward indicator remains whether the March sessions produce a revised tentative agreement package that members will ratify, or whether the parties instead shift toward escalation pathways (e.g., seeking stronger NMB process movement).

What CrewSignal Is Watching

  • Any public confirmation of issue resolution on the remaining “major” quality‑of‑life items (especially scheduling/reserve and fatigue‑adjacent rules).
  • Whether management continues to link improvements to concession trades, and whether CWA-AFA signals any shift in its “no-offset” posture.
  • Whether the March dates are sufficient—or whether the parties add/extend sessions.
  • Any formal NMB step suggesting movement toward release/cooling-off (none observed this week).
