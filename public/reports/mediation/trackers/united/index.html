United / CWA-AFA Mediation

Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14065 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher

Key Takeaways

  • United Flight Attendants entered 2026 still in National Mediation Board mediation after United and CWA-AFA failed to ratify the first 2025 tentative agreement.
  • The May 2025 tentative agreement established a meaningful economic baseline, but the July 29, 2025 rejection made clear that pay gains alone were not enough to carry the deal.
  • United’s 2025 Form 10-K says the company and CWA-AFA continue negotiating a revised tentative agreement.
  • Public reporting in early March 2026 suggested the parties were close to a revised agreement, but no revised tentative agreement had been publicly confirmed as of 2026.03.24.
  • The newest public question is whether a scope concession is part of the final tradeoff. CrewSignal treats that as an important public issue, not as a confirmed settled term.
  • Under the Railway Labor Act, the dispute remains in mediated bargaining. No public release from mediation and no strike deadline have been identified.

Background & Context

United’s Flight Attendant agreement has been open for amendment since August 2021. The National Mediation Board docketed the case in December 2023, and the mediation file was reassigned to Michael Kelliher in November 2024.

Public reporting through 2025 consistently showed the same core issues: base pay, retro pay, compensation for more time at work, scheduling flexibility, reserve life, and broader work-rule improvements. Because the case covers one of the industry’s largest Flight Attendant groups, even modest movement in bargaining posture matters well beyond United.

The 2025 Tentative Agreement and Rejection

On May 23, 2025, Reuters reported that United and CWA-AFA had reached a tentative agreement. Public coverage described the deal as delivering major first-year economic gains, including retroactive pay, and the company later said the package also included scheduling, reserve, and other quality-of-life improvements.

That first tentative agreement did not hold. Reuters reported on July 29, 2025 that United Flight Attendants rejected the deal by a wide margin. United’s 2025 Form 10-K says the company had already recorded a $561 million ratification-related charge tied to the proposed agreement and that the parties continued negotiating a revised tentative agreement after the rejection.

That matters for this tracker because 2025 established both the floor and the problem: a viable second deal likely needs to preserve much of the economic framework while solving the quality-of-life and structural objections that drove the “no” vote.

2026 Year-to-Date

Early 2026 has looked like a revised-TA campaign rather than a release campaign. Public commentary in March suggested the parties were closer than they had been since the failed 2025 vote, and outside reporting described a live possibility of reaching a revised tentative agreement during the March mediation blocks.

The most consequential new public issue in mid-March was scope. In this context, scope means the contract language that protects which flying stays on the United Flight Attendant seniority list and limits shifting that flying to affiliates or alter-ego operations. If that language were loosened, the possible ramification would be greater room for United-owned or United-controlled regional flying outside the United Flight Attendant list. CrewSignal treats that as a potentially important issue if changed, while remaining careful not to report any such change as confirmed without a documented agreement.

As of 2026.03.24, the best conservative read is that United remains in late-stage but unfinished mediated bargaining. Public reporting has pointed to momentum, but not to a publicly confirmed revised tentative agreement.

Status Now

  • Process posture: Active mediation continues.
  • Agreement posture: The 2025 tentative agreement was rejected, and a revised TA remains unconfirmed in public sources.
  • Main watch items: documented revised TA language, formal company or NMB process shifts, and any confirmed resolution of the scope question.
