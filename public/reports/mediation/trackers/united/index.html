United / CWA-AFA Mediation

Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14065 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher

Key Takeaways

  • United Flight Attendants remain in National Mediation Board mediation after the failure of the first 2025 tentative agreement.
  • The May 2025 tentative agreement established a meaningful economic baseline, but the July 29, 2025 rejection showed that members wanted more than headline pay gains.
  • United’s 2025 Form 10-K says the company and CWA-AFA continue negotiating a revised tentative agreement.
  • Early March 2026 public reporting suggested the parties were closer to a revised deal, but no revised tentative agreement had been publicly confirmed as of 2026.03.24.
  • The most consequential new public issue in mid-March 2026 is whether a scope concession could be part of the final tradeoff.
  • Under the Railway Labor Act, the dispute remains inside mediated bargaining. No public release from mediation and no strike deadline have been identified.

2025 Review

United Flight Attendants entered 2025 still in NMB mediation after filing for federal mediation in 2023, with years of frustration over pay, boarding compensation, scheduling, reserve life, hotels, and quality-of-life protections.

On May 23, 2025, United and CWA-AFA announced a tentative agreement covering roughly 28,000 Flight Attendants. Public reporting described the package as delivering major economic gains, including higher pay and other compensation improvements.

That agreement did not hold. On July 29, 2025, members rejected the tentative agreement by a wide margin. Through late 2025, the post-rejection strategy centered on trying to preserve the economic floor from TA1 while repairing the issues that had driven the no vote, especially scheduling, reserve rules, hotels, benefits, and enforceability.

2026 Year-to-Date

The 2026 posture has looked more like a revised-TA effort than a release campaign. Public reporting in early March described the parties as close to a revised contract, but the public record had not confirmed a finished package by 2026.03.24.

The most important new public question is scope. In this context, scope is the contract language that protects which flying stays on the United Flight Attendant seniority list and limits shifting that flying to alter-ego or controlled affiliate operations. If that language were loosened, the possible ramification would be greater room for United-owned or United-controlled regional flying outside the United Flight Attendant list. CrewSignal treats that as a potentially significant issue if changed, while remaining careful not to report any such change as confirmed without documented agreement language.

As of 2026.03.24, the best conservative read is that United remains in late-stage but unfinished mediated bargaining.

Status Now

  • Process posture: Active mediation.
  • Agreement posture: 2025 tentative agreement rejected; revised TA still unconfirmed in public sources.
  • Main watch items: documented revised TA language, formal company or NMB process shifts, and any confirmed resolution of the scope question.
