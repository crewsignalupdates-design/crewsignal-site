PSA / CWA-AFA Mediation

PSA Airlines Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14072 · Mediator: Kevin Barry Last updated: 2026.03.24

Key Takeaways

  • PSA’s Flight Attendant mediation case was docketed by the National Mediation Board in January 2024.
  • In September 2024, public reporting showed PSA Flight Attendants voted 99.2% to authorize a strike. CrewSignal treats that as a pressure signal, not a legal strike trigger under the Railway Labor Act.
  • On 2026.02.19, PSA and CWA-AFA reached a tentative three-year agreement with notable economic and work-rule improvements, including an immediate 10% wage increase and boarding pay.
  • Public reporting on 2026.03.07 said the membership rejected that tentative agreement, returning the file to an unresolved posture.
  • The National Mediation Board’s FY2026 budget justification still lists PSA among the flight-attendant cases that remain in mediation.

Background & Context

PSA entered federal mediation in early 2024 under NMB docket A-14072. By fall 2024, the public record showed a bargaining fight that had moved well beyond ordinary direct negotiations. AirlineGeeks reported in September 2024 that PSA Flight Attendants had overwhelmingly authorized a strike after months of rejected proposals and were returning to the table under NMB oversight.

That vote mattered politically, but not procedurally. Under the Railway Labor Act, a strike-authorization vote is a symbolic pressure signal unless and until the Board exhausts mediation and releases the parties under the statute’s formal sequence.

From Tentative Agreement to Rejection

On 2026.02.19, public trade coverage reported that PSA and CWA-AFA had reached a tentative three-year labor agreement. Public summaries described an immediate 10% wage increase, boarding pay that added an estimated 15% to 16% to compensation on average, retro pay, additional wage-scale increases, scheduling improvements, and greater flexibility. The public ratification timeline ran through 2026.03.06.

That agreement did not become the operative contract. Public reporting on 2026.03.07 said PSA Flight Attendants rejected the deal. As a result, PSA should not be described as a ratified or implemented agreement case as of 2026.03.24.

Status Now

PSA remains best read as an unresolved mediation file. The key public milestone in early 2026 was real, but it failed at ratification. Since then, CrewSignal has not identified a company filing, NMB process change, or notable non-union/public report that clearly maps the next bargaining step.

  • Process posture: Mediation remains the best current public read.
  • Agreement posture: Tentative agreement reached, then rejected.
  • Main watch items: renewed mediated scheduling, a revised package, or a documented company or NMB process update.
