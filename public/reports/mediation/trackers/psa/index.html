TA vs Current Contract

Finding: Based on publicly posted summaries and materials, the tentative agreement reflects substantive change (economics + work rules), not just repackaging / minor adjustments.

  • Pay: Public materials describe an immediate wage increase plus additional wage scale raises across the term.
  • Boarding / ground pay: Public materials describe new boarding pay as a material additive to compensation.
  • Duty rigs / credit: Public communications state minimum day carve-outs are removed at ratification, creating immediate credit impact.
  • Scheduling / flexibility: Public summaries highlight increased flexibility including a 40-hour trade floor and the ability to trip drop.
  • Reserve: Public summaries highlight reserve improvements including automatic check-in/check-out and the ability to pick up trips from lineholders.
  • Hotels: TA FAQ states commuter hotels remain available at the current monthly allowance.
  • Duration / amendability: Public materials describe a shorter-term agreement (three-year).
  • Retro / pay implementation: Public reporting includes retro pay and references an implementation agreement that governs sequencing and effective dates.

For detailed effective dates and enforcement/grievance language changes (if any), treat the full TA language and the implementation agreement as the source of truth.
