Background & Context

Horizon Air Flight Attendants are represented by AFA-CWA under the Railway Labor Act. When direct bargaining does not yield a voluntary agreement, the National Mediation Board can facilitate negotiations through formal mediation. Mediation is process-driven and non-binding: any agreement must still be voluntarily reached and ratified by the membership.

This page establishes a neutral, year-end baseline for tracking Horizon’s mediated negotiations over time. As additional public milestones become available, the timeline below can be expanded without changing the page’s core structure.

2025 Timeline Signals

Crew Signal’s focus is on observable process signals that correlate with progress toward agreement:

  • Cadence: whether bargaining/mediation dates become more frequent or stall.
  • Scope: whether open items narrow (convergence) or broaden (divergence).
  • Posture: whether the mediator’s role intensifies, remains routine, or shifts toward potential release discussions.
  • Ratification risk: whether pay, scheduling, reserve life, hotels, benefits, and enforcement language are being resolved in a way likely to clear a membership vote.

Through 2025, Horizon remained in the mediated phase of bargaining. Weekly updates will document changes in cadence, scope, and public posture as they occur.
