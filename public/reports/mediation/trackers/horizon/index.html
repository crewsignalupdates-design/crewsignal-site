Horizon / CWA-AFA Mediation

Flight Attendants · CWA-AFA

NMB Docket: A-14102 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher

Key Takeaways

  • Horizon Flight Attendants became amendable on 2024.04.30 and were placed into National Mediation Board mediation on 2025.01.24.
  • Alaska Air Group’s 2024 Form 10-K says Horizon Flight Attendant negotiations were ongoing as of the filing date.
  • The National Mediation Board’s FY2026 budget justification continues to list Horizon among flight-attendant cases that remain in mediation.
  • Public non-union reporting on the substance of Horizon bargaining remains unusually thin compared with other airline mediation cases.
  • March 2026 public reporting points to ongoing frustration and public pressure, not to a tentative agreement, ratification vote, or release from mediation.

Background & Context

Horizon Air is a regional subsidiary within Alaska Air Group. Alaska’s 2024 Form 10-K says Horizon Flight Attendants were amendable as of 2024.04.30 and that negotiations for an updated agreement were ongoing as of the filing. In January 2025, the National Mediation Board opened docket A-14102 for Horizon Air Industries Flight Attendants represented by CWA-AFA.

Early public coverage of the mediation move focused on compensation and regional-airline standards. Outside reporting described Horizon Flight Attendants as seeking materially better pay, benefits, and scheduling protections, while also pointing to Alaska’s control of Horizon as an important backdrop to the dispute.

Why This Case Has Been Hard to Read

Horizon is a good example of a real mediation case with limited public visibility. The public record confirms the amendable status, the NMB docket, and the continuing mediation posture, but it does not provide the same steady stream of company filings, trade coverage, or detailed third-party bargaining analysis seen in some larger disputes.

That limited visibility matters. It means CrewSignal should describe the case as unresolved and active, while avoiding stronger claims about pace, package shape, or near-term settlement unless those claims are backed by non-union/public reporting or formal process changes.

2026 Year-to-Date

As of 2026.03.24, the best public evidence still points to unresolved mediation. The NMB’s FY2026 budget justification lists Horizon among the flight-attendant cases that remain in mediation, and outside March 2026 reporting described informational picketing in Portland tied to drawn-out negotiations and low pay.

What is still missing is just as important: CrewSignal did not identify a non-union/public report of a tentative agreement, a ratification calendar, or a release from mediation. That makes Horizon a live but low-visibility mediation file rather than a confirmed breakthrough case.

Status Now

  • Process posture: Active mediation.
  • Public visibility: Thin, with few notable non-union/public bargaining-specific references.
  • Main watch items: a documented tentative agreement, formal NMB process shift, or broader third-party reporting that clarifies whether the parties are converging or stalling.
