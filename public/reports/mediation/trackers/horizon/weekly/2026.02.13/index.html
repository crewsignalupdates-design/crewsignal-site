Weekly Mediation Update

Horizon Air (CWA-AFA)

Week ending 2026.02.13

NMB Case: A-14102 · Mediator: Michael Kelliher

Status This Week

No public indication of a release from National Mediation Board (NMB) mediation, and no announced cooling-off timeline. The primary public development this week was a combined CWA-AFA negotiating committee update covering late January and early February bargaining activity, including a three-day early-February session and a detailed summary of progress and sticking points.

What Changed (Public Signals)

  • Sessions recap (late Jan + early Feb): The committee reported a January session in Chicago (Jan 21–23) and an early-February session (Feb 2–4), with the mediator joining virtually on Feb 4 after two days conducted without in-person mediator participation.
  • Contract language progress: The committee reported meaningful movement in scheduling language (Article 6), including bid/return-to-duty mechanics and standardized internal bidding deadlines.
  • Economics costing underway: The parties’ economists began costing economic proposals, with the process expected to conclude in the near term—an indicator negotiations are moving into a “full-package” pressure phase where multiple items are treated as economic.
  • Deadlock on specific non-economic protections: The committee described a February deadlock on certain protections (including workers’ compensation appointment-related protections), emphasizing the difference between enforceable contract language and unilateral company policy.
  • Management commitment flagged: The committee reported that management committed to present a “meaningful” comprehensive proposal at the start of the next session, including a new pay proposal.

CrewSignal Assessment

This week’s signal is less about visible “NMB action” and more about the underlying bargaining phase shift: economics costing is active, and both sides are positioning for a comprehensive management package at the next session. That combination typically increases the value of member engagement and also clarifies whether talks are moving toward a workable framework—or toward extended mediation with slower movement.

What CrewSignal Is Watching Next

  • Whether management delivers the promised comprehensive proposal (including pay) at the next session.
  • Whether economics costing concludes and becomes a shared baseline for closing issues.
  • Any mediator-driven change in cadence (additional dates, longer sessions, or structured working groups).
  • Whether any “policy vs. contract language” disputes convert into draft article-level tentatives.
