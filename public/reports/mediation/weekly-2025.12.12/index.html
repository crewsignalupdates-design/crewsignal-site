Week of 2025.12.12

United and AFA returned to mediated bargaining under the National Mediation Board, with multiple days of talks completed and further sessions scheduled into January.

It is prepared for informational purposes for crew and labor-focused readers and should not be interpreted as legal advice, bargaining guidance, or a prediction of negotiation outcomes. 1. United–AFA Mediation Status

United Airlines and the Association of Flight Attendants-CWA (AFA) met in mediated negotiations from December 9 through December 12 under the supervision of the National Mediation Board (NMB). The sessions marked a continuation of active bargaining following earlier tentative agreement discussions earlier in 2025.

As of the close of the week, neither party announced a new tentative agreement, and the NMB did not release the parties into a 30-day cooling-off period. Mediation therefore remains ongoing under the Railway Labor Act.

Public communications from the union confirm that bargaining continues on unresolved contract sections. Consistent with RLA confidentiality rules, no detailed proposal language or package terms have been publicly disclosed by either party.

Additional mediated sessions have been scheduled for January 6–9, indicating that the NMB continues to view the dispute as suitable for further facilitated negotiations rather than escalation.

2. Other Active Airline-Industry Mediation Cases

All cases below are publicly confirmed through union communications and National Mediation Board dockets. Public disclosure of detailed bargaining positions remains limited across the industry.

Allegiant — ALPA (Pilots)

Mediation remains active, with ALPA continuing to identify scheduling stability, duty-day structure, and compensation alignment as broad categories in dispute.

Hawaiian — ALPA (Pilots)

Hawaiian pilots remain in NMB-supervised mediation with economic items still open. No release or tentative agreement was reported this week.

Mesa — ALPA (Pilots)

Mesa Airlines continues in mediated negotiations addressing pilot retention, compensation structure, and operational stability concerns.

PSA Airlines — ALPA (Pilots)

Mediation remains active, with union updates continuing to reference staffing and fatigue-related work-rule categories.

Envoy Air — IAM (Fleet & Ground)

IAM-represented employees at Envoy remain in NMB-mediated negotiations, with economics and workplace standards unresolved.

Other Open NMB Dockets (Quiet This Week)

  • Piedmont / ALPA (Pilots). Mediation remains open with limited public updates.
  • Alaska / ALPA. NMB involvement continues on certain implementation-phase matters rather than a new bargaining cycle.

3. Late-December / Early-January Outlook

United–AFA remains the most closely watched flight-attendant mediation currently before the NMB. The scheduling of additional January sessions suggests continued engagement rather than impasse, though no clear timeline for a tentative agreement has been established.

Across the broader airline sector, mediation activity remains steady but subdued, with few public signals of imminent releases or cooling-off periods as the year draws to a close.

This report relies exclusively on publicly available information, including union communications and National Mediation Board procedural disclosures. Under the Railway Labor Act, in-room mediation discussions are confidential unless the parties elect to release details publicly.
