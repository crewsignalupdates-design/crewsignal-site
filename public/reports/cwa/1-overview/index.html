CWA–AFA · Governance Series

Overview

A brief orientation to the series and the structural questions it answers for flight attendant representation.

Purpose

This series provides a modular, source-first reference explaining how flight attendant representation through the Association of Flight Attendants (AFA) operates within the constitutional and structural framework of the Communications Workers of America (CWA).

CWA is a union of unions representing hundreds of thousands of members across multiple sectors and crafts. AFA functions as a national sector within this federation, with its own internal governance model that differs in important ways from CWA’s geographic district system.

What This Series Covers

  • Structural axes: how sectors and districts organize governance in CWA.
  • Districts vs. sectors: why AFA is seated as a sector outside district intermediation.
  • Authority and constraints: how the CWA Constitution frames autonomy in practice.
  • Governance and representation: how representation flows LEC → MEC → International.
  • Funding and dues flow: how dues move through the system and how filings can verify.

This overview is intentionally brief. Each report is written to stand alone as a reference module.
