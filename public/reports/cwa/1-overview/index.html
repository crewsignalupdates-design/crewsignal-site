Overview

The Communications Workers of America (CWA) is a federated labor union representing hundreds of thousands of members across multiple sectors and crafts in the United States and Canada. It operates as a union of unions, combining geographically based districts with industry-specific sectors.

The Association of Flight Attendants (AFA) functions within this structure as a national industrial sector. Unlike most CWA affiliates, AFA is not organized through CWA’s geographic district system. Instead, it operates through a carrier-based, nationwide governance model designed to reflect the operational realities of the airline industry.

Historical Context

The Association of Flight Attendants was founded in 1945 as an independent organization representing airline cabin crew during the early expansion of commercial aviation. Over subsequent decades, AFA developed a governance structure centered on carrier-specific representation and system-wide coordination.

In 2004, AFA affiliated with the Communications Workers of America. That affiliation preserved AFA’s internal constitution, representational pipeline, and bargaining architecture, while situating the organization within CWA’s broader constitutional framework. The result was a hybrid model: an autonomous, industry-specific sector operating inside a larger international union.
