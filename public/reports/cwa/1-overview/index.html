Purpose

This report series provides a structured overview of the Communications Workers of America (CWA) as a federated labor organization, and explains how flight attendant representation through the Association of Flight Attendants (AFA) operates within that broader constitutional framework.

CWA is a union of unions, representing hundreds of thousands of members across multiple sectors and crafts in North America. AFA functions as a national sector within this federation, with its own internal governance model that differs in important ways from CWAâ€™s geographic district system.

The sections that follow examine this structure in detail, focusing on how authority, funding, and representation are organized for flight attendants.
