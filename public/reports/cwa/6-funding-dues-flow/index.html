Funding and Dues Flow

How member dues move through the system — what each layer retains, what it funds, and how filings can be used to verify.

1. The Foundational Detail

AFA dues are currently $50 per month. Critically, AFA dues are not a percentage of gross wages — a defining feature of AFA’s model. Unlike most unions where dues scale with income, AFA uses a flat-rate approach.

This section focuses on structure and flow. Detailed verification using LM filings is handled in the Filings collection.

2. Dues Flow Upward

Dues flow upward from local councils to AFA–CWA and then to CWA National via per-capita structures. While exact allocations can vary by policy and time period, the distribution is commonly described in approximate ranges.

Approximate Retention by Level (Conceptual)

Level Funding Source Approx. Retention Primary Use (High-Level)
Local Unions / LECs $50/month member dues ~20–25% Representation, casework, local operations, member-facing support
AFA–CWA (Sector / International) Per-capita from locals ~45–50% Industry-wide bargaining, safety programs, legal, communications, mobilization
CWA National Per-capita from sectors ~25–30% Organizing, political/legislative programs, audits, national staffing

These ranges are presented as a simplified conceptual model for readers. Verification and year-by-year detail will be documented using filings.

3. Why Retention Matters

Retention ranges matter because they shape the capacity of each layer to respond to member needs. Local retention affects day-to-day representation. AFA retention affects national bargaining and industry programs. CWA retention funds national infrastructure, organizing, and political power.

This report series will later compare how different unions allocate resources across local representation, national bargaining, and enforcement capacity — using primary filings wherever possible.

4. Verification Path

The most direct verification tools for funding and expenditures are the Department of Labor LM filings: LM-2 (larger organizations), LM-3, and LM-4 (smaller organizations). CrewSignal will maintain a dedicated filings library with direct links and summarized extracts.
