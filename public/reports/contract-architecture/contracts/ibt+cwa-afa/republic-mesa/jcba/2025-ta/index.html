Republic / Mesa Flight Attendants

Tentative Joint Collective Bargaining Agreement (Mid-Term)
Shared Representation: IBT Local 135 & CWA–AFA

This page preserves the unions’ joint tentative agreement summary reached following the Republic/Mesa merger. The agreement is explicitly structured as a mid-term JCBA, using the Republic contract as the base while protecting Mesa Flight Attendants during transition.

The agreement preserves the parties’ path to full Railway Labor Act Section 6 negotiations beginning in January 2027, with an amendable date of October 12, 2027.

Comparative Timing Context

Notably, the Republic/Mesa tentative JCBA was reached approximately three weeks after the merger transaction closed. While post-merger bargaining timelines vary by carrier, scope, and legal posture, this compressed timeframe stands in contrast to prior industry JCBA processes.

Following the 2011 United/Continental representation election, the combined Flight Attendant group operated for several years before a joint collective bargaining agreement was presented for ratification. CrewSignal highlights this contrast to illustrate how representation structure, inter-union coordination, and bargaining scope can materially affect post-merger timelines.

This comparison is offered as contextual analysis of process efficiency rather than as a judgment regarding substantive contract outcomes.
