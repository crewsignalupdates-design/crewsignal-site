TWU · Allegiant Air

Contract Analysis

Effective Period: April 15, 2024 – April 15, 2029
Amendable since April 15, 2029

Agreement Metadata

This analysis examines the collective bargaining agreement between Allegiant Air, LLC and the Transport Workers Union of America (TWU) covering Flight Attendants employed by the Company. The agreement became effective April 15, 2024, and runs through April 15, 2029 pursuant to the Railway Labor Act. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

The agreement represents a mature second-generation contract with detailed provisions governing scheduling, reserve utilization, reassignment authority, premium pay, and enforcement. It embeds explicit economic pricing of flexibility rather than relying primarily on grievance-based correction. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Contract Architecture Overview

Allegiant’s TWU agreement reflects a constraint-and-pricing architecture. Operational flexibility is permitted, but it is consistently bounded, sequenced, and monetized through defined multipliers, footprint limits, and frequency caps. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}

Implication Summary: This contract disciplines behavior through cost and transparency rather than prohibition, reducing reliance on post-hoc enforcement.

Scheduling & Assignment Framework

Scheduling is governed by PBS line construction with minimum days-off guarantees, followed by tightly controlled open-time, TDY, reschedule/reroute, Mission Mode, and junior assignment subsystems. Footprint logic and sequencing rules materially constrain ad hoc reassignment. :contentReference[oaicite:12]{index=12}

Economic Structure

Compensation is tightly integrated with scheduling authority. Mission Mode pricing, escalating junior-assignment multipliers, footprint penalties, and comp-day cash-outs function as automatic, self-executing constraints on flexibility. :contentReference[oaicite:13]{index=13}

Enforcement & Dispute Resolution Architecture

Formal grievance and System Board mechanisms remain corrective, but practical enforcement is front-loaded through economic self-execution and union access to operational data. :contentReference[oaicite:14]{index=14}

Structural Strengths, Weaknesses & Comparative Flags

Allegiant’s agreement is a pricing-forward, data-aware contract that constrains repeat behavior through cost and visibility rather than categorical prohibition.
