Scheduling & Assignment Framework

The extension makes narrow but meaningful changes rather than redesigning Endeavorâ€™s scheduling system. The most important scheduling-adjacent change is the â€œ25-in-5â€ provision: if line construction produces a five-day trip, the Flight Attendant receives at least twenty-five hours of pay credit for that trip, though not schedule credit. This is a compensation floor grafted onto the existing line-building architecture.

LOA 20 also changes the training language for new-aircraft certification flying. The Company continues to train and qualify Flight Attendants on Company-operated aircraft based in domicile, but the LOA creates an exception for certification flying tied to a new aircraft type. That preserves management selection discretion in a narrow operational setting while imposing advance notice to the Association.

Economic Structure

The extensionâ€™s largest impact is economic. It replaces the base Section 18 pay appendix with a new rate table beginning in the November 2022 bid month and continuing with scheduled increases through April 1, 2027. It also raises per diem and introduces a geographic-stipend framework of up to $300 per month at designated bases.

At the same time, the geographic stipend remains discretionary by design: it can improve value at targeted bases, but it is not a universal contractual guarantee across the system.

Enforcement & Dispute Resolution Architecture

LOA 20 does not rewrite the grievance, System Board, or discipline framework. Its enforcement impact is indirect: notice requirements tied to the geographic stipend create bounded implementation rules, and the obligation to publish an integrated CBA improves practical enforceability by reducing the risk that Flight Attendants are relying on an out-of-date base PDF without the modification layer.

In other words, the extension strengthens the contract mostly through economics and status clarity, not through a new remedial subsystem.

Structural Strengths, Weaknesses & Comparative Flags

Strengths: clear duration change through March 31, 2027; immediate and scheduled wage gains; higher per diem; a defined compensation floor for five-day trips built in line construction; and modest notice-based constraints around geographic stipend deployment. Weaknesses: the stipend is discretionary, commuter issues are expressly left for further discussion, and the underlying agreement remains procedurally dense.

Comparative flag: this is best read as a targeted uplift and extension package, not a renegotiated contract architecture. It improves the current economic package and clarifies status, while leaving Endeavorâ€™s high-process, high-auditability structure largely intact.
