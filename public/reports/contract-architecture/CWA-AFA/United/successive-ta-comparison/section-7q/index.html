Section 7 — Scheduling

Current CBA p.59TA1 p.77TA2 p.72

Status: Still one of the decisive sections; improved in places, but not fundamentally reset.

Section 7 remains central because it governs how lineholders actually experience loss of flight time, reassignment, trip protection, and open-flying control. TA2 appears to preserve much of the broader TA1 scheduling architecture, with some targeted refinements, but it does not amount to a wholesale rebuild. The section still leaves dense language around reassignment and practical ambiguity that skeptical readers were already focused on.

Why it matters: Scheduling language determines whether contractual value is usable. Even when pay improves, broad company flexibility or dense loss-of-flight-time rules can materially change how that value is actually experienced. That is why Section 7.Q remains a report centerpiece rather than a side note.

Assessment: Mixed. TA2 keeps major scheduling architecture in place and does not fully resolve the reassignment clarity problem.

Adjunct — Section 7.Q Reassignment Explainer

Current CBA Section 7 p.59TA1 Section 7.Q p.104TA2 Section 7.Q p.96

Section 7.Q is still one of the most operationally consequential and reader-sensitive parts of the TA. TA2 carries forward a dense loss-of-flight-time and reassignment structure that determines what happens when pairings cancel, misconnect, duplicate, or otherwise become unavailable. TA2 appears to absorb the old standalone 7.Q.5 settlement-letter architecture into the body text rather than leaving it as a separate LOA, but the result is still legally dense.
