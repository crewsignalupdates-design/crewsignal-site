Section 8 — Reserve Scheduling Procedures

Current CBA p.91TA1 p.123TA2 p.114

Status: Real improvement over the current baseline, but still below the best-in-class reserve model.

TA2’s RAP-based reserve structure is a real improvement over the older all-day-liability model, and the second TA appears to make narrow but meaningful improvements over TA1 as well. Even so, reserve remains heavily dependent on later implementation and still does not approach the cleaner liability rules and stronger monetization that make Southwest a benchmark in member discussions.

Why it matters: Reserve is a ratification-driving issue because it affects daily control, contact windows, assignment reach, and the pay consequences of overreach. TA2 is better than the current baseline, but it still leaves United with long liability windows, 24-hour availability by preference, and narrower remedies than the strongest comparator models.

Assessment: Meaningful improvement, but still well short of the Southwest standard and still heavily tied to JIT / later implementation.

Reserve versus Southwest, plus national reserve snapshots

Current CBA Section 8 p.91 TA1 Section 8 p.123 TA2 Section 8 p.114 TA2 reserve implementation p.385 Southwest reserve guarantee p.100 Southwest RIGs p.177

United’s RAP structure is real and materially better than a pure all-day uncertainty model. Even so, Southwest remains the more useful reserve benchmark because Southwest’s reserve system is not only cleaner on contact windows and assignment boundaries, but is also monetized directly in TFP. Southwest guarantees reserve pay at 6.5 TFP per reserve day, with a monthly reserve guarantee ranging from 78 to 110.5 TFP, and then layers pairing RIGs on top when reserve actually flies. United’s reserve improvements matter, but they still sit inside an hourly pay architecture plus separate add-pay items rather than the more integrated reserve-pay model Southwest uses.

Best formulation

United TA2 modernizes reserve relative to the current operating baseline, but it still does not approach Southwest’s best-in-class reserve model. The difference is not just scheduling cleanliness; it is compensation architecture too.
