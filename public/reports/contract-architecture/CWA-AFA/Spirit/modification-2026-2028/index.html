Contract Modification Information (2026–2028)

Base Agreement: April 14, 2023 – January 1, 2026 · Modification effective January 1, 2026 · New amendable date: January 1, 2028

Agreement Metadata

This page is a document-focused modification overlay for Spirit’s 2023–2026 Flight Attendant Agreement. It is intended to be read alongside the baseline Spirit contract analysis page and the base CBA PDF. The primary modification document is Letter of Agreement No. 2 dated November 24, 2025.

  • Baseline analysis: Spirit Contract Analysis
  • Base CBA: CBA 2023–2026
  • Primary modification document: LOA No. 2 (Nov. 24, 2025)

Modification Summary

LOA No. 2 amended the 2023 CBA as a bankruptcy-era concession package, extended the agreement for an additional two-year term, and established date-certain restoration points in 2028 and 2029 rather than leaving restoration to open-ended future bargaining.

Provision Original / Prior Rule Modified Rule Effective Jan. 1, 2026 Restoration Timing
Overtime incentive pay 1.5x hourly rate above 85 credited hours Reduced to 1.0x hourly rate above 85 credited hours Aug. 1, 2028: 1.25x above 95 hours; Jan. 1, 2029: fully restored
Multi-day pairing guarantee 4:30 per duty period Reduced to 4:00 per duty period July 1, 2029
Additional layover pairing guarantee One extra duty period at 4:30 for each layover exceeding 22 continuous hours One extra duty period at 4:00 for each layover exceeding 24 continuous hours July 1, 2029
Ground holding pay Ground holding pay in force under Section 3.M Eliminated July 1, 2029
Holiday pay 200% of the greater of actual or scheduled credit time Reduced to 150% July 1, 2029
Per diem cap Indexed under Section 4.D.2 Per diem may not exceed $2.99 per hour July 1, 2029
Lead-in conflicts / Day Off Restoration MDD / MDO could preserve pay in certain cases All Day Off Restoration becomes unpaid; MDO no longer used July 1, 2029
Duration Amendable Jan. 1, 2026 Extended for an additional two-year term New amendable date: Jan. 1, 2028

Additional LOA Terms

  • The Company represented that the annualized labor savings in its September 17, 2025 business plan would be shared by other employee groups and that furlough savings would not count toward concession valuation.
  • The Company also represented that management and non-Flight Attendant employees would not receive compensation specifically tied to the successful completion of this LOA.
  • Except as amended by LOA No. 2, the remaining provisions of the 2023 CBA stayed in full force and effect.

Approval and Bankruptcy Context

Public filings indicate that the parties reached agreements in principle in early November 2025, the AFA membership ratified the package on December 11, 2025, and the Bankruptcy Court approved entry into the amended labor conditions on December 29, 2025. The documents below provide the cleanest public paper trail for that sequence.

  • Bankruptcy Conditions Ballot – Certified Results
  • Bankruptcy Doc 603 – Notice of Presentment for Motion to Enter New Labor Conditions Agreements
  • Bankruptcy Doc 910 – Later filing identifying ECF 585 as granted on Dec. 29, 2025 as ECF 642
  • Bankruptcy Doc 668 – Later service filing labeling ECF 642
  • Spirit 2025 Annual Report / 10-K
  • Spirit Q3 2025 10-Q
