Architecture Map

  • Recognition / Scope / Successorship: Article 1 (includes merger protection, wet lease, subcontracting)
  • Definitions: Article 2
  • Pay & Credit System: Article 3 (credit-hour drivers; premium pay; guarantees; incentives)
  • Duty-Time Expenses: Article 4 (lodging, transportation, parking options, per diem)
  • Hours of Service: Article 5 (rest, duty limits, legs/block limits, days off, reschedule limits)
  • Lineholder Scheduling / PBS / SAP / Open Time: Article 6
  • Reserve System: Article 7 (classifications, callout, assignment order, APSB/airport standby)
  • Training: Article 8
  • Sick Leave / Attendance / PDO: Article 9
  • Medical Exams / Fit for Duty Disputes: Article 10
  • Vacation: Article 11
  • Uniforms: Article 12
  • Seniority / Transfers / Furlough & Recall: Articles 13–16
  • Moving / Hostage / Benefits: Articles 17–19
  • Governance & Enforcement: Articles 20–24 (management rights; dues; grievance/SBA; no strike)
  • Safety / Health / Security: Article 26
  • Duration: Article 27

Comparative Notes

  • Scope & successorship are front-loaded: Article 1 is unusually expansive (merger protection + wet lease/subcontracting guardrails).
  • Credit drivers are explicit: pay credit is computed trip-by-trip using block vs duty % vs TAFB % vs minimum daily averages, selecting the “greater of” method.
  • Reserve is a full subsystem: reserve classifications (AM/PM/ER) + airport standby (APSB) mechanics and premium triggers are detailed and rule-dense.
  • Operational flex is structured: AV days, footprint-based rescheduling, SAP, and open time rules form a coherent “control system” for disruptions and coverage.
