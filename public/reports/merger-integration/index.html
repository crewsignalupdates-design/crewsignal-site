Merger / Integration Report

Year-to-Date Summary — Through November 7, 2025

Executive Summary

JetBlue and United announced the Blue Sky collaboration on May 29, positioning it as a loyalty, slot-access, and operations-coordination partnership—not a corporate merger. DOT completed its review on July 29, permitting implementation. Key integration signals include reciprocal frequent-flyer earn/burn (expected Fall 2025), interline expansion, and a JFK↔EWR slot/timing exchange (JFK access for United beginning 2027).

Both carriers publicly deny any merger plan, but the scope of coordination warrants continued monitoring. Labor remains a gating factor: United Flight Attendants rejected a mediated TA on July 30, triggering an internal reprioritization process. No DOJ merger or antitrust filing is on record as of this report.

Integration vs. Merger — Why It Matters

Blue Sky qualifies as integration: loyalty reciprocity, interline expansion, operational coordination, and limited schedule adjustments. These actions do not merge corporate entities, operating certificates, or seniority lists.

A merger would require DOJ antitrust review, NMB single-carrier proceedings, and negotiation of joint labor agreements. Given JetBlue’s blocked acquisition of Spirit, antitrust sensitivity remains high.

Integration Signals to Watch

  • Loyalty: TrueBlue ↔ MileagePlus earn/burn activation (Fall 2025 target).
  • Slots: United receives up to 7 daily JFK round-trip slots starting 2027; JetBlue receives 8 timing positions at EWR.
  • Operational Plumbing: Interline/cross-booking build-out across apps and web platforms.

Labor Context — United Flight Attendants (AFA-CWA)

TA Rejection (July 30, 2025)

United Flight Attendants voted 71% to reject a mediated Tentative Agreement, with over 92% turnout. AFA emphasized that the proposal did not meet members’ expectations on compensation, scheduling, or quality-of-life improvements.

Because the TA was reached during federal mediation, the parties remain in the NMB mediation framework. A rejection does not reopen direct bargaining; only the NMB can alter mediation status.

Post-Vote Survey (Aug 19–Sep 3)

To prepare for the next mediated session, the AFA United MEC conducted a structured membership survey:

  • Opened: August 19, 2025
  • Closed: September 3, 2025
  • Anonymous and confidential
  • Included written responses
  • Evaluated economic, scheduling, reserve, and quality-of-life priorities

As of November 7, AFA has not published numerical results. The MEC states only that member feedback will shape the next bargaining package and define AFA’s position when mediation resumes.

Late-October Discussions (Outside Mediation)

In late October, United and AFA engaged in at least one round of issue-focused discussions without the mediator present. These were clarifying conversations rather than formal bargaining and are permissible under the Railway Labor Act as long as mediation status remains unchanged.

As of this report’s cutoff date (Nov 7, 2025), no new mediated sessions were scheduled and no revised tentative agreement framework had emerged.

Labor Implications for Blue Sky Integration

Labor stability at United remains a moderating factor for Blue Sky’s operational roll-out. Staffing grids, reserve rules, and scheduling flexibility influence the reliability of cross-booking, day-of-operations coordination, and potential schedule harmonization at hubs like EWR, JFK, and BOS.

The post-vote survey and October discussions indicate constructive engagement, but until mediation resumes with a revised proposal, United’s labor climate remains stable but unresolved, which may affect the pace of certain integration elements through late 2025 and early 2026.
