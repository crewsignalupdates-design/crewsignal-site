Merger Integration Comparator

AirTran / Southwest

Editor's Note: This comparator uses primary company and agency sources as the load-bearing base, with archived union materials used as secondary context.

Summary

AirTran and Southwest entered their merger with different Flight Attendant representation structures and different contract baselines, but the integration path looks much less like United/Continental's prolonged legacy-book coexistence and much more like a majority-carrier transition built around seniority integration, an interim/bridge agreement for the shrinking AirTran partition, and a representation endgame that ultimately certified TWU on the combined system.

Core first-pass thesis

AirTran / Southwest is useful as a comparator because it appears to be a representation-and-transition story more than a parallel-legacy-books story. AirTran Flight Attendants entered the merger represented by CWA-AFA, under a contract ratified in June 2005 and amendable in December 2008. Southwest Flight Attendants entered represented by TWU Local 556, under an already-established Southwest-side collective bargaining agreement that Southwest said in 2004 covered June 1, 2002 to May 31, 2008.

The AirTran contract baseline was not barebones. AirTran's SEC-filed Flight Attendant Labor Contract made the 2005 amendments effective June 1, 2005, continued the agreement through November 30, 2008, renewed it annually under the Railway Labor Act absent Section 6 notice, and included explicit successorship, merger protections, and Allegheny-Mohawk integration language for Flight Attendants. AirTran's earlier SEC prospectus also confirms the longer prehistory: the AFA contract was ratified in October 1998, became amendable in October 2002, and a 2003 tentative deal was not ratified.

Southwest's side of the baseline looks different. Southwest told investors in 2004 that a majority of its Flight Attendants ratified a contract in July 2004 covering June 1, 2002 to May 31, 2008. By the merger period, Southwest's filings show that newer TWU Local 556 arrangements were already in force, while AirTran remained a separate represented partition inside the combined company.

Merger and seniority architecture

Southwest announced that a tentative seniority integration agreement had been reached between TWU 556, CWA-AFA Council 57, and the company on December 26, 2011. The company said the agreement would integrate the two Flight Attendant seniority lists and go to both memberships for ratification. The same release said Southwest had finalized the AirTran acquisition on May 2, 2011.

On January 31, 2012, Southwest announced that the Flight Attendants of both carriers had ratified the Seniority Integration Agreement. The company said the agreement integrated the two seniority lists, described that vote as a crucial first step in the integration process, and noted that TWU represented about 9,800 Southwest Flight Attendants while CWA-AFA represented close to 2,400 AirTran Flight Attendants.

Southwest's third-quarter 2011 Form 10-Q adds an important pre-ratification detail: in September 2011, TWU 556 and AFA reached a process agreement regarding seniority list integration, which established the policies and procedures they would follow to reach seniority integration. That is important because it suggests the Flight Attendant path was structured and negotiated relatively early, rather than left open-ended.

Interim / bridge agreement for the AirTran partition

The next important difference from United/Continental is that the AirTran Flight Attendants did not simply remain on a legacy contract indefinitely while a full joint agreement stalled. Southwest announced on September 3, 2013 that Southwest and AirTran Flight Attendants had reached a tentative agreement. Southwest described that agreement as incorporating rewards and incentives for productivity and attendance while remaining mindful of AirTran's low-cost structure.

Southwest's 2013 Form 10-K then says the parties had negotiated an interim collective bargaining agreement for AirTran Flight Attendants that would be effective until affected AirTran employees were transitioned to Southwest, and that, under the seniority list integration agreement, the transition to Southwest was to be completed no later than January 2015. That language is the cleanest proof that the AirTran Flight Attendants remained a distinct transition group, but under a specifically negotiated interim framework rather than an open-ended unresolved status.

Representation endgame

The representation endgame is one of the strongest comparator points. The National Mediation Board's FY2016 annual report says that, after determining Southwest and AirTran were operating as a single transportation system for Flight Attendants, the Board considered an application involving the former Southwest Flight Attendants represented by TWU under an NLRB certification and the former AirTran Flight Attendants represented by CWA-AFA under an NMB certification.

The Board noted the numerical imbalance between the two groups and that the organizations had agreed the application should proceed. The NMB then certified TWU for the combined craft or class and extinguished CWA-AFA's certification. The Board's own determination index lists the matter as R-7457, Southwest Airlines and AirTran Airways, TWU/CWA-AFA, Flight Attendants in May 2016.

That makes AirTran / Southwest especially useful as a comparator because the representation issue appears to have been brought to closure through a defined single-carrier / certification path, rather than lingering for years as a parallel-books system.

Operational endgame

By Southwest's 2014 disclosure, the company said its more-than-three-year integration of Southwest's and AirTran's networks, fleets, systems, and people was effectively completed in December 2014, and AirTran's final passenger service occurred on December 28, 2014. Southwest separately described that final day as the sunset of the AirTran brand.

So, in broad form, the AirTran / Southwest Flight Attendant path looks like this:

  • AirTran entered with an CWA-AFA contract baseline that already contained successorship and merger-protection language.
  • Southwest entered with an established TWU Local 556 contract baseline.
  • The parties negotiated a process agreement in 2011, then a ratified seniority integration agreement in early 2012.
  • The remaining AirTran Flight Attendant partition then operated under an interim agreement pending transition to Southwest, with transition targeted for completion no later than January 2015.
  • The representation question ended with TWU certification on the combined system in 2016.

Comparator Significance

This is a different merger-management pattern from the Continental / United case. In Continental / United, the legacy United and legacy Continental structures remained operationally live for years and were explicitly referenced in the eventual JCBA implementation process. In AirTran / Southwest, by contrast, the Flight Attendant story appears to be one of majority-carrier transition, negotiated seniority integration, interim terms for the shrinking legacy partition, and then single-carrier TWU certification. That makes AirTran / Southwest a valuable comparator for how a non-CWA-AFA merger moved from separate representation into a single-system end state.
