Weekly Update

Alaska / Hawaiian

2026.02.13

Status Summary

This week’s primary integration signal was a pre-merger Hawaiian SEA domicile vacancy and process update, including a negotiated process (LOA) and a posted bid/award timeline for permanent SEA positions effective May 1, 2026.

CrewSignal continues to treat “crew mobility” (who can bid/operate which flying) as a JCBA-dependent milestone. Published union guidance reiterates that, until JCBA ratification and implementation, pre-merger Alaska and pre-merger Hawaiian flight attendants remain separated and fly separately under their own seniority lists, aircraft, work rules, and schedules.

Key Signals Observed

  • SEA domicile vacancy mechanics (pre-merger Hawaiian). Union communications describe 121 permanent SEA vacancies effective May 1, 2026, with bids closing February 16 (11:59 PM HT) and awards posting February 17 (12:00 PM HT), and reference a Letter of Agreement covering the SEA vacancy bidding process.
  • Explicit separation until JCBA. Guidance reiterates that Alaska flight attendants cannot bid into that Hawaiian SEA domicile and that only Hawaiian flight attendants will operate those groupings/aircraft until JCBA ratification and final integration.
  • Corporate integration milestone remains “behind the scenes.” Alaska/Hawaiian have achieved a single operating certificate while continuing to operate as two brands—important background context, but not the same as a merged flight attendant contract or cross-utilization.

What CrewSignal Is Watching

  • SEA bid/award execution. Whether awards post on schedule, whether the LOA process holds cleanly, and whether any disputes arise around relocation/moving provisions connected to SEA staffing.
  • JCBA sequencing signals. Any publication of tentative agreements by section, major work-rule harmonization moves, or explicit messaging about when cross-flying and schedule mixing could begin.
  • Systems/scheduling harmonization. Any evidence that crew-facing scheduling tools or policies are being aligned in ways that precede labor-mobility changes.
