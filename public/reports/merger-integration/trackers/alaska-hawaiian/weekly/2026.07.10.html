Status Summary

Alaska / Hawaiian’s clearest exact-week public signal was combined operating performance. In a July 9 company update, Alaska said Alaska and Hawaiian completed 99.37% of scheduled flights over the July 2 to July 6 holiday period, finished first among U.S. carriers for completion, and recorded three of the ten busiest flying days in company history.

The company also said Alaska Air Group — including Alaska, Hawaiian, and Horizon — ranked first among U.S. carriers in on-time performance for the first half of 2026. CrewSignal reads the disclosure as evidence of coordinated operating execution across the group, while noting that no new exact-week public JCBA, representation, or labor-integration milestone surfaced during the week ending July 10.

Integration Dashboard

  • Alaska said Alaska and Hawaiian completed 99.37% of scheduled flights over the July 2 to July 6 holiday period.
  • Alaska said the two brands finished first among U.S. carriers for completion during the holiday period.
  • Alaska said July 6, July 2, and July 5 were three of the ten busiest flying days in company history.
  • Alaska said Cirium reporting placed Alaska Air Group first among U.S. carriers in on-time performance for the first half of 2026.
  • The company attributed the operating result to employees working across flight decks, gates, cabins, ramps, scheduling, maintenance, and support functions.
  • No new exact-week public JCBA, representation, or labor-integration milestone was identified after the joint flight-attendant negotiating update published on July 3.

CrewSignal Watch Points
