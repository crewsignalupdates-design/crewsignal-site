Status Summary

Republic / Mesa spent the week ending May 15, 2026 in a quieter but still representation-heavy phase. CrewSignal did not identify a new company integration press release or a newly posted NMB weekly report covering the exact week. The strongest current public markers therefore remain Republic’s April 29 statement that Mesa integration will take 18 to 24 months and the NMB’s recent May 1 and May 8 representation entries.

Those NMB entries matter because they continue to show a joint-representation architecture rather than a fully settled post-merger labor map. The May 1 NMB activity report lists a Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group representation application naming IBT / AFA-CWA, AFL-CIO for 2,390 flight attendants, while the May 8 report adds a BLET application for a small Republic / Mesa Air Group craft and still shows the Republic mechanics election under IBT scheduled for a May 28 count. For CrewSignal’s watch item, that means the clearest public evidence of continued joint representation between IBT and AFA-CWA remains intact.

Integration Dashboard

  • Republic’s April 29 results said the first quarter of 2026 was the first full quarter of operations as a combined company with Mesa.
  • Republic said it made significant strides during the quarter in integrating finance, accounting, and human resources functions.
  • Republic said Mesa operations integration is expected to be a multi-year endeavor over the next 18 to 24 months.
  • The NMB’s April 27–May 1 weekly activity report lists CR-7275, dated May 1, 2026, for Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group and identifies IBT / AFA-CWA, AFL-CIO with approximately 2,390 flight attendants.
  • The NMB’s May 4–May 8 weekly activity report lists R-7685, dated May 8, 2026, for Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group and identifies BLET for 4 engine and train service employees.
  • The same May 4–May 8 NMB report still lists the Republic mechanics election under IBT, with a ballot count date of May 28, 2026.
  • The exact reporting week did not produce a fresh company or independent public marker that altered the representation picture established by those recent NMB entries.

CrewSignal Watch Points

  • Watch whether CR-7275 turns into election or certification activity that further clarifies the durable public structure of joint IBT / AFA-CWA representation on the flight-attendant side.
  • Watch for the May 28 mechanics election count and whether it reshapes the craft-by-craft representation map around the combined carrier.
  • Watch for the first concrete public milestones on line-operations integration beyond back-office support functions.
