Status Summary

Republic / Mesa produced a clear representation milestone in the week ending May 29, 2026. The National Mediation Board’s FY2026 determinations page now lists a May 29 certification in Republic Airways for IBT mechanics and related employees, converting the craft from earlier election activity into a completed certification event.

More broadly, Republic still describes Mesa integration as a multiyear 18-to-24-month effort, and the strongest public markers of continued joint representation remain on the flight-attendant side. The NMB’s April 27–May 1 weekly activity report lists Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group flight attendants under IBT / AFA-CWA, AFL-CIO, while the May 4–May 8 report adds a separate BLET application for a small Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group craft. Taken together, the public record continues to show a craft-by-craft representation map rather than a single labor endpoint.

Integration Dashboard

  • Republic said on April 29 that the first quarter of 2026 was its first full quarter of operations as a combined company with Mesa.
  • Republic also said it made significant strides in integrating finance, accounting, and human resources functions and expects Mesa operations integration to be a multiyear effort over the next 18 to 24 months.
  • The NMB’s May 18–May 22 weekly activity report listed the Republic Airways mechanics election under IBT as underway with a May 28, 2026 ballot count date.
  • The NMB’s FY2026 determinations page now lists a May 29, 2026 certification in case R-7676 for Republic Airways IBT mechanics and related employees.
  • The NMB’s April 27–May 1 weekly activity report lists CR-7275, dated May 1, 2026, for Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group and identifies IBT / AFA-CWA, AFL-CIO for approximately 2,390 flight attendants.
  • The NMB’s May 4–May 8 weekly activity report lists R-7685, dated May 8, 2026, for Republic Airways / Mesa Air Group and identifies BLET for four engine and train service employees.
  • These public markers continue to show IBT and AFA-CWA appearing together on the flight-attendant side, while other crafts and classes proceed through their own case-specific representation channels.
  • The exact reporting week did not produce a new broad company integration announcement, but it did produce the most concrete mechanics representation outcome yet visible in the public docket trail.

CrewSignal Watch Points

  • Watch whether the May 29 mechanics certification accelerates labor sequencing in adjacent Republic / Mesa crafts or classes.
  • Watch whether CR-7275 progresses into election or certification activity that clarifies the durability and practical scope of public joint IBT / AFA-CWA representation for Republic / Mesa flight attendants.
  • Watch whether future NMB actions begin to unify more crafts around Republic / Mesa or continue to reinforce a slow, craft-by-craft transition alongside the company’s 18-to-24-month integration timeline.
