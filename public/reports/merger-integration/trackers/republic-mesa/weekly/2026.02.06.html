Republic / Mesa

2026.02.06

Status Summary

Republic/Mesa remains in a post-ratification implementation phase for the joint agreement. MesaAFA and CWA-AFA communications confirm ratification by the combined Mesa/Republic flight attendant group. No new contract modifications or implementation delays were observed this week.

Key Signals Observed

  • Joint bargaining authority under the Merger Transition Agreement (MTA): Public union communications confirm that the Merger Transition Agreement executed between Republic, Mesa, AFA, and the IBT established an expedited, coordinated bargaining framework for a joint flight attendant agreement.
  • IBT as a primary bargaining driver: The pace and structure of negotiations reflect an IBT-led bargaining cadence, consistent with Teamsters-negotiated regional airline agreements and documented in IBT Local 135 materials.
  • Unified ratification and implementation: The JCBA was ratified by the combined Republic and Mesa flight attendant group under joint representation, with post-ratification communications emphasizing stability and coordinated implementation rather than carrier-specific rollouts.
  • Post-ratification phase centered on implementation signals: With the JCBA now in effect, near-term integration signals are focused on interpretation, side letters, implementation guidance, and any early disputes affecting pay, scheduling, or assignment provisions.
  • Contract baseline alignment (“majority absorption”): Public explanations of the JCBA indicate alignment with an existing contract framework rather than the creation of a fully bespoke agreement—an integration pattern commonly observed when a larger bargaining unit sets the operative template under a joint representation structure.
