Republic / Mesa — Weekly Integration Update

Week ending: 2026.02.20

Status This Week

  • The combined group ratified a Joint Collective Bargaining Agreement (JCBA); implementation work continues.
  • Key economic structure includes boarding pay using the 50% standard formula; implementation timing is tied to payroll/technology readiness.
  • Parties have publicly emphasized that full Section 6 negotiations remain scheduled to begin in January 2027.

Process Signals

  • Union implementation materials emphasize seniority integration validation, roadshows/briefings, and a staged rollout of changes that require systems programming.
  • Public summaries highlight scheduling process pilots (e.g., adjustment/trading windows) and reserve transparency mechanisms as part of the transition.
  • Continued publication of tools (summaries, calculators, implementation updates) is a “work is happening” signal even when employer-facing milestones are not publicly announced.

CrewSignal Assessment

  • Integration risk is concentrated in implementation sequencing: payroll programming, scheduling system alignment, and seniority list integration processes.
  • Watch for firm “effective dates” and training/briefing cadence increases; those are typical precursors to operational cutover steps.
  • Monitor whether the initial JCBA changes set a stable floor ahead of the January 2027 Section 6 cycle, or whether significant open issues remain deferred.
