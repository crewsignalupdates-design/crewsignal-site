Allegiant / Sun Country

Week ending 2026.05.08

This Merger & Integration hub tracks airline consolidation signals, commercial partnerships, and representation-level implications. Content is analytical and informational in nature and does not constitute investment advice, merger prediction, or legal guidance. CrewSignal distinguishes carefully between commercial partnerships, operational integrations, closed mergers, and representation-level single-carrier considerations.

Status Summary

Allegiant / Sun Country delivered the clearest transaction milestone of the week ending May 8, 2026. Stockholders at both Allegiant and Sun Country approved the merger-related proposals at special meetings held May 8, effectively clearing the principal remaining shareholder condition after April’s DOT milestone and moving the transaction into final closing preparation.

The more nuanced story now is labor and representation sequencing. Public preparation is asymmetric. The Teamsters are openly preparing members for eventual single-carrier and one-CBA questions, while the most visible TWU-related posture remains procedural and contract-based rather than campaign-style. CrewSignal did not identify a fresh standalone TWU merger-preparation bulletin in the exact reporting week. Instead, the clearest TWU signals remain the transaction FAQ’s process language, the company’s statement that dispatcher issues tied to the future SOC location will be negotiated with TWU as appropriate, and the durability of existing TWU agreements.

Integration Dashboard

  • On May 8, Allegiant stockholders approved the share issuance proposal tied to the Sun Country transaction by 15,997,541 votes for, 34,204 against, and 28,874 abstentions.
  • On May 8, Sun Country stockholders approved adoption of the merger agreement by 43,971,505 votes for, 32,926 against, and 39,103 abstentions; the non-binding compensation proposal also passed.
  • Allegiant’s April 30 first-quarter results still described closing as expected as early as mid-May, subject to satisfaction or waiver of the remaining customary conditions.
  • The transaction FAQ says current unions continue to represent employees until the applicable CBA and federal process change that arrangement, and says joint CBAs typically begin only after the National Mediation Board makes a single-carrier determination and resolves any representation disputes.
  • In a May 4 letter, the Teamsters told members there were no signs yet that either airline had taken the steps necessary for a single-carrier determination, while also preparing technicians and material specialists for eventual one-CBA consolidation once operations are combined under a single operating certificate.
  • The same FAQ says the planned system operations control center will be in Las Vegas and that dispatcher impacts will be negotiated with TWU as appropriate. Sun Country’s annual report shows dispatchers are under a TWU agreement amendable in February 2030, while Allegiant flight attendants are already covered by a five-year TWU agreement.

CrewSignal Watch Points

  • Watch for a formal closing notice and whether the previously announced as-early-as mid-May timetable holds.
  • Watch for the first concrete FAA or NMB step that would turn abstract representation questions into an active single-carrier process.
  • Watch whether IBT and TWU move from process framing into detailed post-close representation strategy once the transaction is no longer just pending.
